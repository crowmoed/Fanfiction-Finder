# Tuning the Ranker Without Going Insane

The ranker has been rewritten three times. Every rewrite started with "the
current scores feel wrong" and ended with "the new scores feel wrong in a
different way." This is the eternal condition of building a ranker.

The current version blends a semantic similarity score from the embedder, a
metadata match score (fandom, tags, characters), and a quality prior derived
from kudos, hits, and chapter count normalized by age. The weights are
constants at the top of the file. They are constants because every time I made
them configurable I spent more time tuning the config than improving the
results.

The hardest part of tuning a ranker for fanfiction is that there is no ground
truth. There's no clickthrough log, no human-labeled "this is what the user
wanted" dataset. You write a query, you look at the top ten, you decide
whether they look right. Then you tweak a weight and look again. Then you
forget what the original results were and convince yourself the new ones are
better.

What helped: a fixed set of about twenty test queries with rough expectations
written down. Not labeled scores - just "for this query, fics tagged X should
appear and fics tagged Y should not." When a tuning change broke one of those
expectations, the change got reverted. When it didn't break any, it was at
least not a regression.

That's the bar. Not a regression.
